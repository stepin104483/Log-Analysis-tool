## 2. Analysis of Filtered Bands (No Anomalies)

### B46 (LTE LAA) - Filtered at NV_Pref

**Root Cause:** Intentional NV item configuration disabling LAA.

**Technical Context:** 
- LAA operates in 5GHz unlicensed spectrum
- Requires Listen-Before-Talk (LBT) and specific regulatory certification
- Often disabled for carriers not deploying LAA or in regions with restrictions

**Impact:** None - expected configuration choice.

### n12, n14, n30 (NR SA) - Filtered at HW_Filter

**Root Cause:** Hardware SKU limitation - RF front-end doesn't include these band modules.

**Technical Context:**
- n12 (700 MHz), n14 (700 MHz FirstNet), n30 (2300 MHz) require specific PA/LNA/filter components
- Different device variants ship with different RF configurations
- RFC defines the superset; HW filter narrows to actual hardware capability

**Impact:** None - hardware correctly reports its capabilities.

### n12, n14, n25, n30, n48, n70 (NR NSA) - Not in RFC EN-DC

**Root Cause:** These bands are configured for SA-only operation in the RFC.

**Technical Context:**
- EN-DC (EUTRA-NR Dual Connectivity) requires specific RF architecture support
- Some bands may lack the simultaneous LTE+NR operation capability
- n48 (CBRS) and n70 are commonly SA-only in many configurations
- n25/n30 being SA-only is unusual but valid for specific implementations

**Impact:** None - architectural decision in RF configuration.
